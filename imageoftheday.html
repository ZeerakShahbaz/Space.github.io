Image of the Day
Nasa posts an Image of the Day page every day. Here are 3 of my favourite images:

Zeta Ophiuchi

Zeta ophiuchi is a hot start that is 20 times larger than the Sun, and its distance from the Earth is around 440 light-years. Zeta ophiuhci formery shared a tight orbit with some other star, but when this companion was obliterated ina supernova explosion over a milllion years ago, Zeta Ophiuchi was catapulted at a speed of roughly 100,000 miles per hour.

 

 

Celestial Cloudscape

This Celestial cloudscape is located in the Orion Nebula. Thousands of stars are developing in the turbulent dust and gas area known as the Orion Nebula. It is one of the most observed regions of the night sky and frequently serves as a target for the Hubble space telescope since it is the nearest zone of massive star formation to Earth.

 

 

Small Magellanic Cloud

A tiny galaxy called the Small Magellanic CLoud circles our own Milky Way galaxy at a distance of around 200,000 light-years. It is one of the Milky Way's nearest galactic companions. Although the Small Magellanic Cloud is so tiny that it is known as a dwarf galaxy it is so bright that it can be seen with the unaided eye, meaning without a telescope, from both the Southern Hemisphere and areas close to the equator.
